AI Should Not Be Banned in Schools

Artificial Intelligence tools, such as ChatGPT and other generative AI platforms, have quickly become a staple of modern academic life. Ranging from high school and college students who use these tools to help them brainstorm ideas and understand difficult concepts, AI is reshaping education.

While concerns about cheating and overreliance are real, banning AI entirely would ignore its educational benefits and the reality of a rapidly changing technological world. AI should be allowed in schools, but only with clear rules and guidance so that it supports student learning instead of replacing how students think.

The Core Argument

Educators and students face an important crossroad. Instead of focusing solely on the risks of AI, schools should explore the immense potential of artificial intelligence as an instructional partner. By teaching the ethical use of AI, schools prepare students for the real world.

The Value of Innovation

With tools that summarize readings, generate outlines, and offer 24/7 assistance, students can navigate hectic modern schedules more efficiently while focusing on higher-order synthesis and critical thinking.

Pedagogical Benefits and Personalized Learning

One of the major reasons for keeping AI in schools is that it makes learning easier by ensuring learners comprehend complicated concepts. There are cases where learners find themselves struggling to understand complex theories, especially in math, science, and even literature.

Simpler Explanations and Examples

With the help of AI tools, Artificial Intelligence can break information down into simpler explanations, give examples, and provide better explanations based on how students process that information.

According to UNESCO’s Guidance for Generative AI in Education and Research, generative AI can support learning when used responsibly because it offers "age-appropriate," "human-centered," and adaptive assistance that helps students engage with material more effectively. This means AI is not meant to replace teachers, but to give students additional support when they need clarification.

Individualized Feedback and Support

In a systematic review of AI in higher education, Zawacki-Richter et al. found that AI systems help instructors understand student performance and provide individualized feedback, which leads to better learning outcomes. When students are made to know specifically what they need, they tend to understand the lesson rather than just memorize it.

This study proves that AI is more helpful to learners than a conventional classroom setting. This could also be helpful for students who may be too shy to ask questions in class or who need extra time to process information.

Productivity and Time Management

Another great benefit of AI that helps students in school is its ability to help students have an easier time finishing their work and staying productive. Being a college student myself, I am well aware that most of us have a very hectic life because we are growing into adults. We have trouble managing our time, organizing things, and starting to do our tasks.

Drafting and Summarizing

AI tools can make life much easier by helping generate outlines, summarizing long, unnecessary readings, and brainstorming ideas. These features help students begin the thinking process instead of working for them.

U.S. Department of Education Insights

The U.S. Department of Education’s 2023 report highlights how AI can support productivity by helping students "organize information, draft ideas, and receive feedback" in ways that make learning more efficient. In real classrooms, teachers already use AI to help students break down large assignments into smaller steps or to provide quick explanations when teachers are busy helping others.

Career Readiness and Industry Application

The most important reason why the banning of AI must not occur is due to the requirement of AI knowledge by students for their careers in the future. The application of artificial intelligence has expanded in many different industries, ranging from health care, business, engineering, teaching, and even the arts.

Staying Ahead in the Workplace

As seen in the "AI Index Report 2023" by Stanford University, the application of AI in the workplace will continue to rise, and workers with an understanding of AI will definitely gain an upper hand.

Educating the Next Generation

Educating students on the proper usage of AI is not much different from educating them on how to use calculators, computers, or even the internet. Initially, all of these were considered a way out for students to cheat in class and skip work. However, in the long run, schools came up with ways to incorporate these into the learning process positively. AI is only a step forward in technological advancement.

Ethical Policies and Responsible Usage

The opposition to the use of AI in learning institutions says that it needs to be banned since it promotes cheating, spreads false information, and hinders critical thinking. Such fears are justifiable. However, the issues are a result of misuse, and not the technology itself.

UNESCO's Guidance

UNESCO insists that the key is not to prohibit AI technologies, but to develop "ethical, safe, equitable, and meaningful" policies for the use of AI. Likewise, the U.S. Department of Education maintains that it is important to encourage children to learn about using AI technology responsibly instead of banning the technology.

Moving Beyond the Fear

Schools already deal with various forms of cheating, from copying homework to purchasing essays through essay mills to plagiarism online. Banning AI technology will not stop cheating but rather make it invisible. What schools need to do is train students in using AI as an educational tool, not as a substitute for their work.
